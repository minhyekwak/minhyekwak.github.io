Day 1  so that

On my way to subway in this morning, someone was on his phone saying "so that..." and passed me by.

Meaning: Purpose / Result

Two opposite meanings are in one word.

ex) Purpose_ She swims everyday so that she can stay healthy. (source : YBM)
ex) Result_ He stands his friend up so that he have no friends. (source : YBM)

Paradox and irony could be found in this phrase.

Progress: Start = End

This artwork aims to express the conception which started from the attribute of the phrase, "so that." The phrase has already two opposite meaning, purpose and result. Purpose is used before the main event starts, and result usually happens in the last minute of the event. The conception has improved by the idea that start and end are not different, moreover they are in the same position.

Day 2  overthrow

This morning as I turned on random electronic music playlist from Apple music, the first lyric of first song was "overthrow."

Overthrow !== Uphold

Overthrow_ remove forcibly from power: military coups which had attempted to overthrow the king (New Oxford American Dictionary).
Uphold_ confirm or support (something that has been questioned) (New Oxford American Dictionary)

South Korean domestic political situation

Recently South Korea has been through complicated political events. Former president, Park Geun-Hye, was accused by bribe and abuse of authority so that she was impeached last year. President Park was a symbolic person who represent conservative, corrupted, and dictatorial old generation. The new president has been elected on May 2017, President Moon Jae-In, and he is admired and supported by the majority of people of South Korea for his integrity and devotion to the weak. This series of events which contain fall and rise of government is evaluated as achievement of South Korean people who had been continously fighting for upright government. We call it "Candle Revolution."

Board Game: New Generation, New Government

Purpose_ Remind, commemorate, and celebrate of South Korean people's victory.

Description_ Game board represents South Korean map including 8 regions. Each region has 17 engraved circles so that chips can be put on those circles.Chips are separated as red(President Park), blue(President Moon), and yellow(double-value, candle revolution). Cards as well, separated by 3 colors, containing the different number of each President.

How to Play_ Each players are assigned their regions by flipping a coin/rock, paper, scissors. Cards are piled up and nobody can see how many each president are there. Players, one by one, pick a card, check the number of each president, and put chips according to the card they picked. When all of circles are filled, the game is over. The winner is the one who has the highest number of blue chips. The yellow one count as two blue chips.
